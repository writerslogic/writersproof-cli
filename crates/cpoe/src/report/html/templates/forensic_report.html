§ 3 · Methodology and standards basis{{report_id}}
§ 3

Methodology and standards basis

Capture procedure, analytical framework, validation studies
3.1

The WritersLogic CPoE Engine records process evidence during document creation through four sequential stages. Each stage produces evidence that is cryptographically chained to its predecessor such that any subsequent modification invalidates the entire chain. The protocol is specified in draft-condrey-cpoe-protocol-00 [Ref. 6] and the reference implementation is published under an open-source license at the address indicated in Section 10.

3.2.1

Behavioural capture

Keystroke timing, cursor position, revision events, paste operations, and window focus changes are recorded continuously through operating system input application programming interfaces during the period the document is open in a monitored application. Capture occurs at the device level; no content is transmitted to remote services.

3.2.2

Cryptographic anchoring

Document content is hashed under SHA-256 [Ref. 2] at intervals not exceeding sixty seconds and at every paste, focus change, or revision burst. Each checkpoint is bound to its predecessor by HMAC-SHA256 [Ref. 3] under a session key derived from the device signing key. A Verifiable Delay Function [Ref. 7] is applied to the chain head to produce temporal binding that cannot be backdated.

3.2.3

Stylometric and behavioural analysis

Statistical analysis is performed across six independent dimensions: inter-keystroke interval distribution, burst and pause structure, edit and revision pattern, paste-to-typed ratio, writing velocity, and process continuity. Each dimension produces a likelihood ratio comparing the observed evidence under H1 and H2 calibrated against the validation corpus described in paragraph 3.4.

3.2.4

Likelihood ratio combination

Per-dimension likelihood ratios are combined under the Bayesian framework recommended by ENFSI [Ref. 1] using logarithmic summation. Dimensions with measured cross-correlation greater than 0.30 are weighted to avoid double-counting evidence. The combined likelihood ratio is mapped to the ENFSI verbal scale to produce the evaluative statement.

Standards basis and validation

The methodology applies the following standards and validation studies:

  • ENFSI Guideline for Evaluative Reporting in Forensic Science, 2015 [Ref. 1], specifically Sections 2.3 (likelihood ratios) and 4.2 (verbal scale mapping).
  • RFC 8032 (Edwards-curve Digital Signature Algorithm) and RFC 4493 (AES-CMAC) for cryptographic primitives [Refs. 2–3].
  • Internet-Draft draft-condrey-cpoe-protocol-00, presented at IETF 125 SECDISPATCH (Shenzhen, March 2026) [Ref. 6].
  • Validation corpus of 14,820 captured documents (8,420 human-composed, 6,400 transcribed or AI-generated) across six genres. Type-I error rate at the present operating point: 0.4%. Type-II error rate: 1.6% [Ref. 8].
  • Methodology has been disclosed to the C2PA Trust and Provenance Working Group and the Decentralized Identity Foundation.
3.3

The scoring and verdict determination are performed by the deterministic CPoE engine without human judgment. The named examiner of record reviews the cryptographic integrity of the evidence chain, confirms the validity of device attestation, and adopts the engine output as the evaluative statement only where chain integrity is verified. Where chain integrity fails or the captured session is insufficient under the limitations stated in Section 8, the examiner records the result as inconclusive and does not issue an evaluative statement.

§ 8 · Limitations and scope{{report_id}}
§ 8

Limitations and scope

What this report does and does not establish
8.1

This report records cryptographic process evidence and the result of statistical analysis of that evidence. It does not establish the personal identity of any individual present at the keyboard during the captured session. Attribution of the captured behaviour to a specific natural person requires evidence in addition to that recorded here, such as direct observation, biometric authentication, or contemporaneous corroboration.

8.2

What the report supports

The report supports findings as to whether the behavioural patterns observed during the captured session are consistent with real-time human composition or with transcription, dictation, or automated input. Where the cryptographic chain is verified intact, the report establishes that the captured evidence has not been altered since the time of capture and is bound to the originating device by hardware attestation.

8.3

Effect of session duration

8.4

What the report does not support

The report does not establish the cognitive origin of ideas expressed in the subject document, the absence of generative-AI involvement in ideation or drafting prior to the captured session, or any conclusion about the truthfulness, originality, or substantive quality of the subject document’s content. The report is not a determination of plagiarism, academic misconduct, or fraud. Such determinations require evidence and analytical procedures outside the scope of this examination.

8.5

Factors that may affect interpretation

Heavy paste activity, collaborative authorship, document import from external editors, captures conducted across multiple unmonitored devices, accessibility software that modifies keystroke timing, and intentional adversarial behaviour designed to mimic transcription patterns may all affect the per-dimension metrics. The examiner has reviewed the captured session for indications of such factors; none was observed.

8.6

Validation corpus and error rates

The methodology has been calibrated against a validation corpus of 14,820 captured documents across six genres (academic, journalistic, technical, creative, correspondence, and dictation). The reported Type-I error rate (incorrect support for H2 given human authorship) is 0.4 percent and the Type-II error rate (incorrect support for H1 given transcribed or generated input) is 1.6 percent at the operating point used for this examination [Ref. 8]. Error rates are subject to update as the validation corpus is expanded; the values stated reflect the corpus as of report generation.

8.7

Standing of the examiner

The named examiner is the founder of WritersLogic, Inc. and the principal author of the CPoE protocol specification. This standing is disclosed for the avoidance of doubt. The methodology applied is publicly documented and independently reproducible; the present examination produces the same evaluative statement under any conforming implementation of the protocol applied to the same evidence bundle.
