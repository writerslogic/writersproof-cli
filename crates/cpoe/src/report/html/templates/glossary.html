Assessment Score
A composite metric (0–100) derived from all analytical dimensions. Higher scores indicate stronger evidence of human authorship. The score is converted to a likelihood ratio for statistical interpretation. A score of 0 indicates insufficient evidence for evaluation, not a negative finding.
Bigram Consistency
A measure of how stable the timing patterns are between specific pairs of consecutive keystrokes (e.g., "th", "er"). Higher values indicate the author has a consistent, practiced typing style.
Checkpoint
A timestamped record of the document's state at a point during composition. Each checkpoint contains a SHA-256 hash of the document content, an HMAC binding to the previous checkpoint, and a PoSME timing proof. The sequence of checkpoints forms a tamper-evident chain; altering any checkpoint invalidates all subsequent entries.
CPoE (Cryptographic Proof of Effort)
The protocol used to capture and cryptographically bind behavioral evidence during document creation. Specified in Internet-Draft draft-condrey-rats-pop.
ENFSI Verbal Equivalence Scale
A standardized scale for expressing the strength of forensic evidence, published by the European Network of Forensic Science Institutes (2015). Maps likelihood ratios to verbal descriptors: Against (LR < 1), Weak (1–10), Moderate (10–100), Moderately Strong (100–1,000), Strong (1,000–10,000), Very Strong (≥ 10,000). The scale describes evidential weight, not certainty.
H₁ / H₂
The two competing hypotheses under evaluation. H₁ (proponent): the document was composed through a human writing process. H₂ (alternative): the document was produced by transcription, dictation, automated generation, or paste from an external source.
HMAC-SHA256
A keyed-hash message authentication code using SHA-256. Used to bind each checkpoint to its predecessor under a session-specific key, ensuring that the chain cannot be modified without detection. Defined in RFC 2104.
IKI CV (Inter-Keystroke Interval Coefficient of Variation)
The coefficient of variation (standard deviation divided by mean) of time intervals between consecutive keystrokes. Human typing typically produces CV values of 0.30–0.80, reflecting natural rhythm variation. Values below 0.10 suggest automated or replayed input; values above 1.5 may indicate frequent interruptions or multi-tasking.
Likelihood Ratio (LR)
The ratio of the probability of observing the evidence under H₁ to the probability under H₂. An LR of 100 means the evidence is 100 times more probable if H₁ is true than if H₂ is true. An LR of 1.0 is evidentially neutral. An LR below 1.0 favors H₂. The LR does not express the probability that either hypothesis is true; it quantifies the evidential weight of the observations.
Log₁₀(LR)
The base-10 logarithm of the likelihood ratio. Positive values favor H₁; negative values favor H₂; zero is neutral. A log-LR of +2.0 corresponds to LR = 100; a log-LR of −1.0 corresponds to LR = 0.1.
SHA-256
A cryptographic hash function (NIST FIPS 180-4) producing a 256-bit (32-byte) digest. Any change to the input, even a single bit, produces a completely different hash. Used throughout CPoE for document fingerprinting, checkpoint chaining, and integrity verification.
PoSME (Proof of Sequential Memory-bound Effort)
A memory-hard sequential computation that proves a minimum amount of real wall-clock time elapsed between checkpoints. Unlike VDFs, PoSME binds each step to a large memory buffer, making it resistant to GPU/ASIC acceleration. Used in CPoE to prevent after-the-fact fabrication of evidence chains.
