This Examination Supports:

  • Evidence of human cognitive constraint patterns during composition
  • Stylometric and behavioral consistency with natural authorship
  • Documented, reproducible methodology suitable for dispute review
  • Cryptographic chain-of-custody from creation through examination

This Examination Does Not Establish:

  • The identity of the specific author (requires supplementary evidence such as biometric authentication or direct observation)
  • That AI-assisted tools were never used during any phase of writing (the methodology detects process patterns, not tool presence)
  • That the text has not been subsequently edited, paraphrased, or translated after the capture session ended
  • The cognitive origin of the ideas expressed in the document

Factors That May Affect Results:

  • Substantial post-hoc editing or translation of original text
  • Genre transitions (e.g., technical to creative writing mid-document)
  • Use of templates, outlines, or highly structured prompts
  • Collaborative or multi-author composition
  • Accessibility software that modifies keystroke timing (e.g., switch access, eye tracking, voice control)
  • Sessions below minimum evidence thresholds (fewer than 50 keystrokes, under 60 seconds, fewer than 5 checkpoints, or under 20 words)

Evidentiary Standards:

  • Evidence generated by automated, verified process suitable for self-authentication under FRE 902(13)/902(14)
  • Hash chain provides tamper-evident integrity suitable for authentication under FRE 901(b)(9)
  • ENFSI-compliant evaluative reporting per European forensic science guidelines (2015)
  • Methodology disclosed and reproducible; independent verification instructions provided
