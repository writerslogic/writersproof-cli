## Current design (as-built, 2026-06-24)

This section is authoritative for the current badge; everything below it describes the superseded pre-redesign badge.

**Two orthogonal axes.** A badge reads `Mode — Tier`.
- **Authorship Mode** (identity): Human-Authored / AI-Assisted (Disclosed) / Human-Revised. No "Certified AI-Generated" — the engine proves human process; undisclosed AI-pattern composition reads as Human-Revised. Carried in the credential + the accessible `<title>`.
- **Assurance Tier** (level): Verified (hardware-bound) / Corroborated (attested software) / Declared (software-only). Unknown values degrade to Declared.

**Anatomy** (`crates/badge-fingerprint/src/badge.rs`; navy `#16243f`, 300×360):
- Navy **coin ring** — rounded scallops on a smooth annulus.
- **WRITERSPROOF** banner curved inside on the white interior (top arc).
- **Fingerprint** — the real flow-field loop/whorl print, ONE offset element in a fingertip oval (not filling the interior; that reads as an identity badge). Deterministic `f(payload)`.
- Large freestanding **checkmark**, center-right, overlapping the print, white halo.
- **Tier value-ramp**: one navy hue at three intensities (`Tier::ink`) + ribbon word + 3/2/1 **dots**.
- **VERIFIED/CORROBORATED/DECLARED ribbon** at the bottom.
- **Short-code** `WP-XXX-XXX-XXX-C` in monospace below.

**Short-id** (`short_id.rs`): Crockford Base32, **9 payload symbols (45 bits) + 1 mod-37 check** = `WP-XXX-XXX-XXX-C`, from `SHA-256("wp-short-id-v2:"+author_did)`. `payload_from_identifier` is the lookup key; `short_id_from_identifier` is the display form; `validate()` normalizes + checks.

**Trust model (the key decision).** The fingerprint is a **visual commitment**, NOT a data channel. Machine decode = the **badge file** (baked OB3 credential, lossless); human/print decode = the **OCR-safe short-code text** + the print as a visual cross-check. No bits are encoded in the ridges. The credential `id`/verify URL uses the **payload** (`https://verify.writersproof.com/c/{payload}`) — the check symbol can be URL-unsafe (`* ~ $ =`), so it is never in the URL.

**Verification (render==verify):** the portal re-derives the print and channels from the signed payload via the same Rust core (native + wasm) and compares; the OB3 credential carries the data and its Ed25519 / `eddsa-jcs-2022` proof is the cryptographic root.

**Source of truth:** `crates/badge-fingerprint/src/{badge,short_id,features,fingerprint}.rs`; OB3 mapping in `crates/cpoe/src/war/profiles/openbadge.rs`; render examples `examples/dump_badge.rs` + `dump_fp.rs`. See the `badge_design_locked` memory.

---

The badge is a **deterministic, tamper-evident visual commitment** to a signed
credential. It is generated by pure integer / Q16.16 fixed-point math from a
single short-id, so the same id yields a byte-identical SVG on every platform
(native CLI/desktop and `wasm` in the verify portal). The artwork is flat
single-ink navy line-art — **not** metallic, colored, gradient, or 3D — by
design: it must read as a deterministic engineering artifact, not an
AI-generated illustration.

## 1. The two orthogonal axes

A badge encodes exactly two independent facts. They never collapse into one
scale — a badge reads as **"`Mode` — `Tier`"**, e.g. *"Human-Authored —
Verified"*.

### Axis A — Authorship Mode (badge *identity*)

What the achievement attests. Source: `AuthorshipMode` in
`crates/cpoe/src/war/profiles/openbadge.rs`; `Mode` in
`crates/badge-fingerprint/src/badge.rs`.

| Mode | Label | What it means |
|------|-------|---------------|
| `HumanAuthored` | **Human-Authored** | Original human composition, no declared AI assistance. |
| `AiAssistedDisclosed` | **AI-Assisted (Disclosed)** | The author *disclosed* AI-tool use; the witnessed process documents human authorship over that assistance. |
| `HumanRevised` | **Human-Revised** | A human substantially revised pre-existing or imported content. |

**Honest framing — there is no "Certified AI-Generated".** The engine proves a
human *process* and detects deviation from it; it cannot cryptographically prove
that text was authored by an AI. So:

- Author-declared AI use → **AI-Assisted (Disclosed)**. Disclosure is the only
  authoritative source of an AI claim.
- **Undisclosed** AI-pattern composition reads as **Human-Revised** (a human
  working over pre-existing material) — *never* as a fabricated AI disclosure.

**Evidence → mode rules** (`infer_authorship_mode`, openbadge.rs:139):

1. `ai_disclosed == true` → **AI-Assisted (Disclosed)** (overrides every forensic signal).
2. else `composition_mode ∈ {paste_domesticate, paste_veneer, ai_mediated}`, **or** `writing_mode == transcriptive` → **Human-Revised**.
3. else → **Human-Authored**.

### Axis B — Assurance Tier (badge *level*)

How strongly the evidence is bound, i.e. the trust of the *capture
environment* — not a quality ranking of the author. Source: `Tier` in
badge.rs; `tier_label` in openbadge.rs:263.

| Tier | Label | Internal tier string | Binding |
|------|-------|----------------------|---------|
| `Verified` | **VERIFIED** | `hardware_bound` | Signing key bound to hardware (TPM / Secure Enclave). |
| `Corroborated` | **CORROBORATED** | `attested_software` | Evidence signed by an attested software key. |
| `Declared` | **DECLARED** | (anything else) | Software-only signed proof-of-process chain. |

Both axes **degrade safely**: an unknown/forged mode parses to *Human-Authored*
(can never gain AI stars), and an unknown/forged tier parses to *Declared* (can
never inflate assurance). See `Mode::from_slug` / `Tier::from_slug`
(badge.rs:45, badge.rs:67) and their `from_slug_unknown_degrades_safely` test.

## 2. Legend table — Mode × Tier

The full 3×3 identity grid. Every cell is a valid badge; the credential `name`
is literally `"{Mode} — {Tier}"`.

| | **VERIFIED** (hardware-bound) | **CORROBORATED** (attested software) | **DECLARED** (software-only) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Human-Authored** | Human-Authored — Verified | Human-Authored — Corroborated | Human-Authored — Declared |
| **AI-Assisted (Disclosed)** | AI-Assisted (Disclosed) — Verified | AI-Assisted (Disclosed) — Corroborated | AI-Assisted (Disclosed) — Declared |
| **Human-Revised** | Human-Revised — Verified | Human-Revised — Corroborated | Human-Revised — Declared |

Visual deltas across the grid: **tier** changes the dot-row count (3/2/1) and
the ribbon label; **mode** changes the top-left mode glyph and adds stars for
AI-Assisted only. Everything else (frame footprint, banner, checkmark) is
identical.

## 3. Badge anatomy — the visual channels

Canvas is fixed for every badge: a `300 × 360` viewBox, disc centered at
`(150, 150)`, single ink `#16243f` on white. The **frame footprint is
pixel-identical across all ids**; only the per-channel contents vary. Source:
`render_badge_svg` (badge.rs:92).

| # | Channel | Encodes | Deterministic from id? | Source |
|---|---------|---------|------------------------|--------|
| 1 | **Scalloped notary-seal ring** (2 concentric circles + 24 teeth) | Per-tooth **tooth-code**: tall vs short tooth, ~1/3 raised | Yes — `tooth_code[24]` | `scalloped_ring`, badge.rs:157 |
| 2 | **"WritersProof" banner** (per-glyph arc across the top) | Brand; fixed string | No — identical for all | `top_banner`, badge.rs:208 |
| 3 | **Fingerprint** (in the central slot disc, r=70) | The generative print: pattern class, singularities, ridges, minutiae, harmonics — a **visual commitment** (see §4) | Yes — the main individualizing channel | `fingerprint_in_slot` / `emit_fingerprint`, badge.rs:243 |
| 4 | **Checkmark** (circled, bottom-right of disc) | "Attested" mark; fixed | No — identical for all | `checkmark`, badge.rs:337 |
| 5 | **Tier dot-row** (just under the banner) | **Tier count**: Verified=3, Corroborated=2, Declared=1 dots; the whole row slides ±a few px across 8 slots as a small visual checksum | Count = tier (fixed); row offset = `dot_slot` | `dot_row`, badge.rs:361 |
| 6 | **Tier ribbon** (notched banner at disc bottom) | Tier label text: `VERIFIED` / `CORROBORATED` / `DECLARED` | No — driven by tier arg | `ribbon`, badge.rs:452 |
| 7 | **Mode glyph** (top-left, ~`30,36`) | Mode identity: pen-nib triangle (Human-Authored), sparkle (AI-Assisted), revision arrow (Human-Revised) | No — driven by mode arg | `mode_glyph`, badge.rs:411 |
| 8 | **Mode stars** (around the inner ring) | **AI-Assisted only**: 2–3 stars at coarse slots, 4/5/6 points | Yes — `stars[]`; gated to AiAssisted | `stars`, badge.rs:382 |
| 9 | **Short-id text** (monospace, below the disc) | `WP-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX` (see §5) | Yes — it *is* the id | `short_id_text`, badge.rs:479 |

All geometry uses heavy stroke weights and wide dot/tooth spacing **so the
trust-bearing channels survive a downscale to the 0.5″ fax floor** (see §6).

## 4. The fingerprint — a visual commitment

The fingerprint is **not** a stored image and **not** an invertible hash — it is
a one-way deterministic *projection* of the id, designed to be hard to forge
holistically and easy to recompute for comparison. Source:
`crates/badge-fingerprint/src/fingerprint.rs` + `features.rs`.

**Entropy source.** Every channel derives from a single hash:

```
h = SHA-256("fp-v1:" + short_id)
```

The version tag `fp-v1` (`features::VERSION`) is part of the hashed preimage, so
bumping it cleanly re-keys every badge. A small deterministic byte-cursor
(`Bits`) walks `h`, rehashing `SHA-256(buf)` for more entropy than 32 bytes
provides.

**Derived feature vector** (`derive_features`, features.rs:159 →
`FeatureVector`):

| Feature | Budget / range | Constant |
|---------|----------------|----------|
| Pattern class | Loop / Whorl / Arch, flattened to ~even thirds for max glanceable distinctness | — |
| Base rotation | quantized to 64-brad steps | — |
| Singularities (cores / deltas) | placed per pattern (loop: 1 core + 1 delta; whorl: 2 deltas, no core; arch: 1 distant core) | — |
| Warp harmonics | 4 low-amplitude (≈1.4°–4.5°) sinusoids perturbing the field | `HARMONICS = 4` |
| Minutiae | 7 markers — endings (filled cap) + bifurcations (fork tine), oriented along local ridge flow | `MINUTIAE = 7` |
| Tooth-code | 24 per-tooth bold flags, ~1/3 raised | `TOOTH_CODE_LEN = 24` |
| Dot-row slot | coarse angular slot for the row checksum | `DOT_SLOTS = 8` |
| Stars (AI only) | 2–3 stars, slot 0–11, 4/5/6 points | — |
| Fine-ridge mask | u32 selecting decorative/incipient ridges | — |

**Ridge generation.** The orientation field uses the **Sherlock-Monro
zero-pole model**:

```
theta(z) = base_rotation + 0.5 · ( Σ arg(z − delta_k) − Σ arg(z − core_k) ) + harmonics
```

Ridges are streamlines traced perpendicular to `theta` via **evenly-spaced
(Jobard-Lefer) placement**: a seed lattice blankets the slot, each uncovered
seed is traced both directions until it leaves the disc or runs within the
spacing distance (5 slot-units) of an existing ridge. Capped at `MAX_RIDGES =
64`, hard-clipped to the slot disc. The result is a dense field of nested
loops/whorls/arches that fills the seal.

**Why this is a commitment, not decoration:** every visual channel — the
fingerprint *and* the tooth-code, dot positions, stars — is a deterministic
function of the same signed id. A forger cannot tweak one channel in isolation;
to present a different id with matching artwork they must match **every** channel
simultaneously (see §7).

## 5. The short-id `WP-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX`

Source: `crates/badge-fingerprint/src/short_id.rs`.

- **Derived, not invented.** `short_id_from_identifier(author_did)` =
  `SHA-256("wp-short-id-v1:" + identifier)`, first 128 bits read as a big-endian
  `u128`, emitted as **16 base-30 digits** (most-significant first), grouped
  `WP-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX` (22 chars total).
- **Source identifier = the author DID** — the same identifier carried by the
  credential and resolvable at `verify.writersproof.com`. One identity system,
  no parallel id registry.
- **Unambiguous alphabet** (30 symbols): `23456789ABCDEFGHJKMNPQRSTVWXYZ` —
  the look-alikes `0 1 I L O U` are removed, so there is no 0/O, 1/I/L, or U/V
  confusion when read aloud, printed, or faxed.
- **Collision surface:** `30^16 ≈ 2^78.5`; residual modulo bias from `30^16`
  not dividing `2^128` is ≈`2^-49` (astronomically small). It is hash-derived,
  so this is the *only* collision surface.
- **It is a one-way commitment, not id storage.** The full identity lives in
  the rendered short-id text **plus the signed credential** — the badge art
  commits to the short-id, the credential carries the resolvable identity.

DST `wp-short-id-v1:` versions the derivation; bumping it re-keys every short-id.

## 6. Sizing & degradation

- **Minimum 0.5″.** Below that the badge is **"not for verification."**
- **At the fax / photocopy floor the fingerprint drops below its resolution
  floor BY DESIGN.** The fine ridges and minutiae are *meant* to vanish — they
  are the high-fidelity channel.
- **Low-fidelity carriers** (intended to survive 1-bit threshold + dot-gain):
  the tier dot-row count, the ribbon block, and the **short-id text** rendered
  below the badge.

Verified against the rendered gallery
(`crates/badge-fingerprint/gallery/gallery-fax.png`, regenerated 2026-06-24 via
`cargo run -p badge-fingerprint --example gallery`): at the 100px / 1-bit /
dilated fax simulation the fingerprint is gone, the ring breaks into a dotted
circle, the ribbon survives as a **solid block** (label text not reliably
legible), the dot-row survives as scattered marks, and the **monospace short-id
text below the disc is the single most robust carrier**. See
[§10](#10-not-yet-rendered-gaps-vs-concept-art) for the honest caveat on dot-count
legibility and the absent QR.

## 7. The verification loop (render == verify)

The same Rust core renders natively and compiles to `wasm`
(`crates/badge-fingerprint/src/wasm.rs`: `render_badge`, `render_fingerprint`,
`short_id_from_id`, `features_json`). The verify portal therefore **re-derives
the canonical badge from the signed short-id and compares it to the presented
artwork** — render and verify are the same function.

1. The portal reads the signed credential (its `id` is the verify URL carrying
   the short-id; see §8).
2. It re-derives the short-id from the credential's **author DID**
   (`short_id_from_id`) and confirms it matches the value in the credential `id`.
3. It recomputes `derive_features(short_id)` and re-renders every channel
   (`features_json` / `render_badge`) and compares them to the presented badge.
4. It verifies the **Ed25519 `eddsa-jcs-2022` (or COSE) signature** over the
   credential.

**A forger must match _every_ channel** — fingerprint + 24-tooth code + dot-row
positions + (for AI) mode stars + the short-id text — **and** forge the
signature over the credential. Matching the artwork without the signature proves
nothing; forging the signature without matching the deterministic artwork is
detectable channel-by-channel.

**Credential id** = `https://verify.writersproof.com/c/{short_id}`
(`VERIFY_CREDENTIAL_BASE_URL`, openbadge.rs:52, 399).

## 8. Open Badges 3.0 / 1EdTech mapping

Source: `crates/cpoe/src/war/profiles/openbadge.rs`. An EAR token is projected
into an `OpenBadgeCredential` (the well-known alias of `AchievementCredential`),
a conformance-targeted extension of the W3C VC 2.0 projection in
`profiles/vc.rs`.

```jsonc
{
  "@context": [
    "https://www.w3.org/ns/credentials/v2",
    "https://purl.imsglobal.org/spec/ob/v3p0/context-3.0.3.json"   // pinned OB 3.0.3
  ],
  "id": "https://verify.writersproof.com/c/WP-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX", // == credential id == short-id seed
  "type": ["VerifiableCredential", "OpenBadgeCredential"],
  "name": "Human-Authored — Verified",                              // "{Mode} — {Tier}"
  "issuer": {                                                       // Profile OBJECT, not a bare string
    "id": "<issuer_did()>",                                         // did:webvh → did:web fallback (see below)
    "type": ["Profile"],
    "name": "WritersProof",
    "url": "https://writersproof.com"
  },
  "validFrom": "...", "validUntil": "...",                          // validFrom + 365 days max
  "credentialSubject": {
    "id": "<author_did>",
    "type": ["AchievementSubject"],
    "achievement": {
      "id": "https://writersproof.com/achievements/human-authored", // keyed off MODE slug
      "type": ["Achievement"],
      "name": "Human-Authored",                                     // mode label
      "description": "Cryptographically witnessed evidence that a human composed...",
      "criteria": { "narrative": "Awarded at the \"Verified\" assurance tier. ..." },
      "achievementType": "Badge",
      "tag": ["assurance:verified"]                                 // TIER carried as the LEVEL
    },
    "processAttestation": { /* preserved CPoE claims: status, trustVector, documentRef, chainDuration, attestationTier, writingMode, compositionMode, forensicSignals */ }
  },
  "credentialStatus": { "type": "BitstringStatusListEntry", "statusPurpose": "revocation", ... },  // when set
  "evidence": [ { "type": "ProofOfProcessEvidence", "verifier": "...", "sealHash": "..." } ],       // the .cpop ref
  "proof": { "type": "DataIntegrityProof", "cryptosuite": "eddsa-jcs-2022", "proofValue": "f<hex>", "verificationMethod": "<author_did>#key-1", "proofPurpose": "assertionMethod" }
}
```

**Mapping rules:**

- **Mode → Achievement.** Achievement `id` =
  `https://writersproof.com/achievements/{mode-slug}` (slugs: `human-authored`,
  `ai-assisted-disclosed`, `human-revised`). `name`/`description` come from
  `AuthorshipMode::label()` / `::description()`. Each mode is a distinct,
  dereferenceable achievement.
- **Tier → level.** Carried as the achievement `tag` `"assurance:<tier>"` **and**
  in the credential `name` — *not* as a separate achievement. Keeps the two axes
  orthogonal.
- **Securing.** Ed25519 `eddsa-jcs-2022` Data Integrity proof (sig over
  `SHA-256(proof_options_jcs) ‖ SHA-256(document_jcs)`, `proofValue` = multibase
  base16 `f` + lowercase hex) — or a COSE_Sign1 envelope
  (`to_cose_secured_open_badge`, content type `application/vc+cose`, alg EdDSA).
- **Revocation.** `BitstringStatusListEntry` (`set_credential_status`), shared
  with the VC profile.
- **Evidence.** `ProofOfProcessEvidence` referencing the signed `.cpop` packet.
- **Issuer DID** (`vc::issuer_did()`, vc.rs:181): `did:webvh` if the
  `did-webvh` feature + a local identity exist (strongest), else the signed-in
  user's `did:web:writersproof.com:profile:{user_id}`, else the org-level
  `did:web:api.writersproof.com`. The notarizing CA issuer key is the
  pre-provisioned `NOTARIZE_CA` Ed25519 key in the API worker.

### Conformance caveats (in progress)

- ✅ `eddsa-jcs-2022` Data Integrity proof and COSE_Sign1 securing implemented
  and tested.
- 🚧 `eddsa-rdf-2022` and VC-JWT securing mechanisms — **not yet implemented**.
- 🚧 Full 1EdTech conformance suite (extension `@context` for the
  `processAttestation` extension, RDF canonicalization path) — **in progress**.

## 10. Not-yet-rendered (gaps vs concept art)

The current renderer (`render_badge_svg`) produces channels 1–9 in §3 and
**nothing else**. The following appear in concept art or downstream specs but are
**not** emitted by `badge-fingerprint`:

1. **QR code — NOT rendered.** The "how to read a badge" concept sheet and the
   degradation story reference a QR linking to the verify URL, but the crate has
   **no QR dependency** and `render_badge_svg` emits no QR. Any QR must be
   composited externally by the issuing app/portal from the credential `id`
   (`https://verify.writersproof.com/c/{short_id}`). Until then, the §6 claim
   "the QR carries the low-fidelity case" is **aspirational** — in the as-built
   badge the **short-id text** is the QR's stand-in at fax fidelity.
2. **Metallic / color-coded tiers — NOT rendered (intentional).** Concept art
   showed gold/silver/bronze metal with gradients and 3D shadows. The renderer
   is **single-ink `#16243f` on white**. Tier is encoded by **dot count + ribbon
   label only**, never by color. This is deliberate: the badge must not look
   AI-generated and must stay print/fax/grayscale-safe.
3. **Dot-count legibility at the harsh fax floor is weaker than the spec
   implies.** In `gallery-fax.png` the ribbon survives as a solid block but its
   label text is not reliably legible, and the dot-row degrades to scattered
   marks where counting 3 vs 2 vs 1 is not dependable. The **short-id text** is
   the only consistently robust low-fidelity carrier today. Treat ribbon-label
   and dot-count as *crisp-resolution* tier carriers, not fax-resolution ones.
4. **`processAttestation` forensic fields default empty.** `writingMode`,
   `compositionMode`, and `forensicSignals` are `None` unless the caller invokes
   `enrich_forensic_signals`; `credentialStatus` is `None` unless
   `set_credential_status` is called.
